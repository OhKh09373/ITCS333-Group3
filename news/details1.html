UOB Hosts Astronauts for the Launch of 'Al Munther' Satellite

Short description

astronauts shared their space journey marking the launch of the Bahraini satellite Al Munther, and highlighting the role of science, AI, and astronaut training.

Category

Academic Achievement

Details

Emirati astronauts Muhammad Al-Mullah and Noura Al-Matroushi discussed their experience in space, in a virtual interactive meeting held on Wednesday (5 March 2025 AD), at Sheikh Abdul Aziz Hall at the University of Bahrain in Sakhir, on the occasion of the launch of the Bahraini satellite (Al-Munazar).

Among astronauts the importance of investing in the world of space amid the tremendous global evolution in the fields of advanced sciences, including artificial intelligence, which is currently actively employed in the service of satellites.

Attendance recognizes the most important preparations, physical, and psychological of astronauts before going on their space journeys. Emphasize the importance of the appropriate scientific specialty for space sciences, for joining the team of astronauts, which include various engineering, physics, geology and other related sciences, as well as the ability to work within a team of different nationalities, languages and cultures.

Participate in the virtual meeting - organized by the Bahraini National Authority for Space Sciences and the Mohamed Bin Rashid Space Center in the United Arab Emirates, - students of the University of Bahrain, students of a number of private universities, as well as students of a few private schools in the Kingdom.
